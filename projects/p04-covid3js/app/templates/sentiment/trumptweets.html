Trump Tweets

Sentiment analysis on tweets from Donald Trump, the president of the United States

The following scatterplot shows the sentiment values of Trump tweets during the COVID-19 pandemic.

The polarity values on the y-axis range from -1.0 to 1.0, inclusive, where -1.0 is most negative and 1.0 is most positive. The range of the x-axis is the time between January 1, 2020 and April 15, 2020.

The following bar chart shows a more discrete perspective of the previously shown data, where different ranges are created (in intervals of 0.2 within the overall range of [-1.0, 1.0]) and the frequency of tweets that belong in each range is recorded.

Each tweet that is less than or equal to the right endpoint of each range is considered to be in that range. For example, if a tweet had a polarity rating of 0.0, we would categorize that to be in the -0.2 to 0.0 range. We see that the majority of the tweets are within the -0.2 to 0.0 range. We also see that the barchart is skewed to the right. This means the majority of the tweets are positive. However, there are many neutral tweets that are rated as 0.0 and are counted in the -0.2 to 0.0 range.
